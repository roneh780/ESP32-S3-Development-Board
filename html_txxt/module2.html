2.2 Detailed Notes and Operation Specifications

2.2.1 Power Supply and Voltage

Power Supply Methods:

  • Default: Use USB Type-C to connect to computer or USB charger (5V) to power the development board.
  • If using Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, or driving multiple peripherals simultaneously, it is recommended to use a 5V/2A external power supply (via USB-C port) to avoid repeated restarts or program anomalies due to insufficient USB power.

Voltage Levels:

  • All GPIO pins of ESP32-S3 are 3.3V logic level.
  • Never directly connect 5V power or 5V signals to any GPIO pin, as this will burn the chip.
  • If you need to connect 5V peripherals (such as certain sensors, buzzer modules), you must use a level conversion module (3.3V ↔ 5V) or ensure the peripheral is compatible with 3.3V input.

2.2.2 Wiring Order

Connect GND first, then power:

  • When connecting any circuit, please connect the GND (ground) wire first, and finally connect the power wire (such as 3.3V or 5V).
  • When disconnecting, first disconnect the power wire, then disconnect GND.
  • This can prevent damage to the development board due to unstable power or accidental short circuits.

Common Ground Requirement:

  • When the development board works with external power supplies and sensor modules, all devices' GND must be connected together, otherwise signals cannot be transmitted normally.

2.2.3 ESD Protection and Operating Environment

Electrostatic Discharge (ESD) Protection:

  • Before touching the development board or any electronic components, touch a metal object (such as a computer case, radiator) to discharge static electricity from your body.
  • It is recommended to operate on an anti-static mat or wear an anti-static wristband.
  • Avoid placing bare boards directly on dry carpets or plastic tables.

Avoid Moisture and Liquids:

  • Do not use or store the development board in humid environments (such as bathrooms, kitchens).
  • If the board accidentally gets wet, immediately power it off and thoroughly dry it before testing.

2.2.4 Wire and Connection Quality

USB Cable:

  • Many cheap USB cables only contain power wires (two wires) and lack data wires (D+, D-), so they cannot be used for program burning and serial communication. Please use a Type-C cable with data transfer capability.

Jumper Wires:

  • It is recommended to use 22AWG or thicker tinned copper wires, avoid using soft or low-quality wires, otherwise the contact resistance will be large and easy to loosen.
  • Ensure that the jumper wire ends are completely inserted into the breadboard or development board pins when plugging.

2.2.5 LED Current Limiting Resistor

  • Each LED must be connected in series with a 220Ω or 330Ω resistor. Never directly connect an LED to a 3.3V or 5V power supply, otherwise the instantaneous current will be too large and burn the LED (or even damage the GPIO port).
  • Calculation reference: The typical voltage drop of an LED is about 2V, when powered by 3.3V, current = (3.3-2)/220 ≈ 5.9mA, which is bright enough and safe.

2.4 Common Errors and Troubleshooting

Symptom Possible Cause Solution
Development board not recognized (no COM port on computer) USB cable only for charging, driver not installed, cable damaged Replace data cable; install CP210x or CH343 driver; check Type-C interface
Program upload failed or stuck at "Connecting..." GPIO0 not properly pulled low (some boards need manual operation), unstable power Hold BOOT button and press RESET once to enter download mode; use external power supply
LED not lit or very dim Resistor too large (e.g., 10kΩ), LED polarity reversed Change to 220Ω~470Ω; long leg is positive (anode) connected to GPIO, short leg is negative connected to GND
Development board frequently restarts Insufficient USB power (especially when Wi-Fi is enabled) Use 5V/2A external power supply; turn off high-power peripherals
Pin output abnormal or heating Pin shorted to 5V or GND; output current too large Immediately power off; check wiring; avoid directly driving motors, relays (need driver board)

2.5 Hardware Checklist (Before Each Experiment)

  • All components are in good condition, no burning, breakage, or pin oxidation
  • USB data cable can normally transfer data (test with mobile phone or other devices first)
  • Development board power supply is normal (red power indicator is on)
  • No 5V signals directly connected to any GPIO pin
  • Each LED is connected in series with a suitable resistor
  • Breadboard and jumper connections are firm, no looseness or short circuit
  • If using an external power supply, confirm its output voltage is 5V and shares ground with the development board
